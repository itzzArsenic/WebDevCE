Who Were the Signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (signed July 4, 1776):

John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; famed for his bold, oversized signature (“put your John Hancock”).
Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence; later the 3rd U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Oldest signer at 70; renowned inventor, diplomat, and statesman.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Leading advocate for independence in Congress; later the 2nd U.S. President.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Key organizer of colonial resistance and revolutionary activism.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign the Declaration, Articles of Association, Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
Only Catholic signer; last surviving signer (died in 1832).
